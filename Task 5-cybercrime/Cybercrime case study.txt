# Cybercrime Case Study: Phishing Attack

Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which attackers try to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted entity.

A common phishing scenario begins with the victim receiving an email or message that appears to be from a legitimate organization, such as a bank, online shopping platform, or even their college. The message often creates a sense of urgency, such as ìYour account will be blockedî or ìYou have won a reward.î

The message contains a link that redirects the user to a fake website designed to look identical to the original. When the user enters their login credentials or financial details, the attacker captures the information instantly.

Students are frequent targets because they regularly use email, online payments, and social media platforms. Many may not verify links carefully, making them vulnerable.

For example, a student received an email claiming to be from a popular payment app asking to verify their account. After clicking the link and entering details, their account was compromised, resulting in unauthorized transactions.

Phishing attacks can lead to financial loss, identity theft, and misuse of personal data. This highlights the importance of awareness and careful verification before trusting any online communication.

# Stay Safe Online ñ Prevention Checklist

## ?? General Safety Tips
- Always verify the sender before responding to emails or messages
- Do not click on suspicious or unknown links
- Check website URLs carefully (look for https:// and correct spelling)
- Use strong and unique passwords for different accounts
- Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) wherever possible
- Avoid downloading files from untrusted sources
- Regularly update your device and apps for security patches

## ?? Financial / UPI Safety Tips
- Never share your OTP, UPI PIN, or bank details with anyone
- Remember: entering UPI PIN means money is being sent, not received
- Do not scan QR codes from unknown sources
- Always verify the receiverís name before making any payment

## ?? Social Media Safety
- Do not accept friend requests from unknown people
- Avoid sharing personal information publicly
- Be cautious of messages asking for money or urgent help
